# Premium Insight Tone Guide

MySky should feel less like a personality app and more like a private observing intelligence that becomes more accurate with history.

## Core promise

- It remembers.
- It notices what repeats.
- It gets more specific with time.
- It names patterns without flattening the person.
- It validates through recognition, not flattery.

## Premium tone

- Deep, but earned.
- Personal, but not invasive.
- Warm, but not syrupy.
- Insightful, but not mystical in a generic way.
- Confident, but never absolute when the signal is still forming.

## What premium copy should sound like

- "A pattern in your data suggests..."
- "This has shown up more than once."
- "From the outside, this may look one way. Internally, it seems to feel another way."
- "The pattern here is not inconsistency. It is sensitivity to context."
- "By the time this feels obvious, it may already have been building for a while."

## What to avoid

- Generic praise: "You are thoughtful and emotionally intelligent."
- Flattening labels: "You are the kind of person who..."
- False certainty: "This is exactly who you are."
- Therapy-sounding filler: "Your authentic self seeks alignment."
- Horoscope vagueness: "You contain many layers and deep truths."

## Insight structure

Each premium pattern should do four jobs:

1. Recognition
2. Interpretation
3. Stakes
4. Reflection

## Generation rules

Every generated premium insight should follow these rules:

- Lead with a pattern, not a personality verdict.
- Prefer `your data suggests`, `a pattern in your entries`, or `this has shown up repeatedly` over `you are`.
- Include one concrete receipt whenever possible:
  day count, recurrence, trend direction, comparison to baseline, or repeated theme.
- Name tension or contradiction when it exists.
  Example: capable on the surface, strained underneath.
- Validate by explaining the pattern's logic, not by flattering the user.
- Use confidence proportionally.
  Strong data can sound direct.
  Early data should sound provisional.
- Keep one useful edge in the copy.
  The sentence should reveal something, not just summarize.
- End with a reflection or next step that is specific to the pattern.

## Rewrite checklist

Before shipping a card, ask:

- Could this apply to almost anyone?
- Does it sound equally polished in every sentence?
- Did we earn the level of certainty?
- Is there at least one signal, contrast, or remembered pattern in it?
- Would a user feel recognized rather than categorized?

Example:

**Stress You Carry Quietly**

You often stay capable after strain has already started building. From the outside, you may still look clear and functional. Internally, your system may already be paying a cost.

Why this matters:
If your stress stays hidden behind capability, you may notice your limits later than you should.

Reflection:
What are the earliest signs that you are still functioning, but no longer truly resourced?

## Language patterns that increase willingness to pay

- Use memory:
  "This pattern has strengthened lately."
  "We have seen this before."
  "Compared with earlier entries..."

- Use nuance:
  "You do not always need the same thing to recover."
  "This looks less like avoidance and more like protection."

- Use dignifying validation:
  "That strength is real. The cost is that you may notice your depletion later."

- Use conditional precision:
  "Early signs suggest..."
  "A stronger signal is beginning to form..."

## UI copy direction

- Prefer `What's becoming clear` over `Today's insight`.
- Prefer `Seen repeatedly` over `Seen in your data`.
- Prefer `Early signal` over `Building picture`.
- Prefer `What your archive is learning` over `What MySky sees`.
- Prefer `Observed across 83 days` over `83 DAYS`.

## Reusable template

Title:
Name the pattern in human language.

Body paragraph:
Describe the pattern as a repeated tension, contradiction, or adaptive behavior.

Signal row:
- Pattern
- Observed across
- Confidence

Callout:
Use `Reflection` when inviting self-observation.
Use `Try this` when the insight implies a practical next move.

## Litmus test

If the user could read the sentence and think "that could describe almost anyone," it is not premium yet.
