# Insight Personalization Examples

This document provides hundreds of examples showing how to make insights deeply personal using specific user data.

## Core Principle
**Generic**: "Your system appears to need rest."
**Personal**: "After 3 nights of poor sleep, your patience drops by 15 points and you mention feeling 'on edge' in your journals. Sleep isn't optional for you—it's calibration."

---

## 1. SLEEP INSIGHTS

### Generic (Before)
"Sleep affects more than tiredness for you."

### Personal (After) - 10 Examples

1. **With sleep quality data**: "After nights rated below 3/5, your next-day stability drops 18 points on average. Your body doesn't just feel tired—it loses its steadiness."

2. **With journal correlation**: "On the 7 days you mentioned 'exhausted' or 'drained' in your journal, 6 of them followed nights with less than 6 hours of sleep."

3. **With mood tracking**: "Your mood averages 6.2/10 after good sleep vs 3.8/10 after poor sleep—a 2.4 point swing that's larger than most life events."

4. **With specific symptoms**: "Poor sleep shows up for you as irritability (mentioned 8 times), brain fog (5 times), and feeling 'fragile' (4 times)—not just tiredness."

5. **With recovery time**: "After a bad night, it takes you an average of 2.3 days to return to your baseline steadiness. One night affects three days."

6. **With best day correlation**: "Your 10 best days all included sleep rated 4/5 or higher. Good sleep isn't just helpful—it's foundational."

7. **With stress amplification**: "On high-stress days, poor sleep doubles the impact. Stress + good sleep = manageable. Stress + poor sleep = overwhelming."

8. **With physical symptoms**: "After poor sleep, you log tension in your shoulders (6 times), headaches (4 times), and heaviness (3 times) before you even mention feeling tired."

9. **With relationship impact**: "On days after poor sleep, you're 3x more likely to mention conflict or feeling 'short' with others in your journal."

10. **With work capacity**: "Your focus and productivity ratings drop from 7.1/10 to 4.2/10 after poor sleep—a difference you can feel but might not connect to rest."

## Implementation Guidelines

### For Each Insight Type:

1. **Always include specific numbers**: "8 out of 10 days" not "most days"
2. **Name the actual ingredients**: "nature walks, therapy, journaling" not "restorative activities"
3. **Show the timeline**: "2.3 days to recover" not "takes time"
4. **Quote their language**: "You mention 'on edge'" not "you feel stressed"
5. **Compare to baseline**: "6.8/10 vs your usual 5.2/10" not "higher than normal"
6. **Show cause and effect**: "After X, Y happens" not "X and Y are related"
7. **Name the people**: "talking to Sarah" not "social connection"
8. **Specify the activities**: "morning walks" not "movement"
9. **Include the exceptions**: "except when" or "but not when"
10. **Show the pattern evolution**: "used to take 4 days, now takes 2" not "you're improving"

### Red Flags (Avoid These):
- "Your system appears to..."
- "This may suggest..."
- "There seems to be..."
- "You might need..."
- "This could mean..."

### Green Flags (Use These):
- "On 8 of your 10 best days..."
- "After nights rated below 3/5..."
- "You mention 'exhausted' 6 times, always after..."
- "Your stability drops from 7.2 to 4.8 when..."
- "This pattern has held for 23 days..."

## The "Wow, This App Knows Me" Test

An insight passes this test when a user thinks:
- "How did it know that?"
- "That's exactly what happens"
- "I've never seen it spelled out like that before"
- "This is eerily accurate"
- "I feel seen"

An insight fails this test when a user thinks:
- "That could apply to anyone"
- "That's pretty vague"
- "I guess that's sort of true"
- "This feels generic"
- "The app is guessing"
